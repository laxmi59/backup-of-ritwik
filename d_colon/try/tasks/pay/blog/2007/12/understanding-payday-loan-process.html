Understanding the Payday Loan Process

You have probably already considered a payday loan or quick cash advance to get you through financial rough patch or to pay for an unexpected emergency medical expense or even to repair something on your car. Payday loans are easily available and can be obtained fairly quickly, most times in the same day. Many of us will see a payday cash advance as an instant life saver, but it's better to know what to expect before applying.

Firstly, make sure you have read and fully understand the terms and conditions. Don't worry too much if you have bad credit or no credit at all. There are plenty of companies out there that offer fair terms to people who might have suffered some financial difficulty in the past. You will however need to prove some form of income to assure the prospective lender that you will be able to repay the cash advance back once the time arises to do so. Some lenders might also require you to fax some additional documents but some lenders will state to be faxless lenders as well which means that almost everything can be done online.

To qualify for just about any loan you would need a steady source of income including payday loans or cash advances.

Don't stress too much about the fact that you might be overwhelmed with the amount of fine print involved. Just keep a few key rules of thumb in mind to help you make some good choices.

  1. Some companies might charge a penalty fee if you choose to repay the loan too early. If you are not aware of the penalty, you could end up paying more than you originally anticipated.
  2. Try finding a company that has no early payoff penalty in place.
  3. If your credit score is good, then you can most likely find a suitable conventional loan at a lower annual interest rate. However, it usually takes a lot longer to be approved and get your money. If you need some cash fast and you think you'll be able to pay the loan back rather quickly, then a payday loan or cash advance may be right for you.

Paying off your loan on time can definitely help build your credit score with lenders. If you take out a payday loan to help you repay another loan or credit card repayment on time, you could avoid a late fee that would have cost you more than you ended up paying in interest on the payday advance. Plus, making a credit card payment late will hurt your credit score even more. Be sure not to borrow more than you can easily pay back by your next pay day. Payday lenders make most of their money off people who can't afford to pay back their loans on time. If you are responsible about it, then a payday loan could really help you out of the financial fix you might be finding yourself in.
