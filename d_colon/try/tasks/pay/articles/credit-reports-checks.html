Credit Reports & Checks – Tracking Your Credit Score

Conducting credit reports/checks regularly helps you avoid being refused a loan. Experian and Equifax are the main agencies that compile credit reports.

What Information Do These Reports Offer?

Credit reports/checks offer information regarding your credit history. You can check county court judgment records, bankruptcy, and credit defaults against your name. You can also find out which loan companies have requested for information on your credit score.

View Records Online

An online credit report helps you view your credit history online. All the information is made available through third parties in collaboration with the credit rating agencies. You can log in using a user name and password, and then scan the credit report. It is safe. You do not have to worry about anyone gaining access to your personal information. If you are pressed for time, then getting information online is recommended. It also helps you get an idea of the ratings that you have got from both the agencies.

Fees

You can take a look at your credit report as many times as you want in return for subscription with an online service provider. Free credit reports are the best way to judge your chances of getting a loan. If you have to apply to more than one agency for a loan at different times, it is not worth paying repeatedly for checking the credit scores.

Which Factors Affect Credit Reports/Checks?

Credit reports/checks are affected by repayment history, current debts, previous loan terms, and types of loans. If your repayment history was good and you did not default on payment, you get a high credit score. A high score increases your chances of getting loans on easier terms and lower interest rates.

Apart from good repayment records, your score becomes high if:

  • You are a fulltime employee with a registered company
  • You own a house
  • You have been residing in your current home for a few years
  • You are a registered voter
  • You do not borrow too often

Note that the credit rating agency does not assign a score. It simply has a record of your credit history. The credit score is assigned by the loan company based on your credit record and information that you provide through the application forms. This is important as many borrowers feel disheartened once they see their credit record. However, the decision to lend to you is based on certain other factors apart from your credit history. If you have access to credit reports/checks, you can understand your credit record and take steps to increase your chances of getting a loan.

Viewing your credit reports/checks before applying for a loan is important, as you can take informed decisions regarding the type of loan, a reasonable interest rate, and the loan company to deal with.
